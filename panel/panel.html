01 — Market Timing

The Polling Industry
Is in Free Fall

Legacy pollsters are retreating, response rates have collapsed to historic lows, and trust in polling data is eroding. The market isn't just ready for disruption — it's begging for it.

🚨 The Gallup Retreat

Gallup has stopped tracking presidential approval ratings — their flagship product for decades — because reaching representative phone samples is now too expensive and statistically unreliable.

📞 The Landline Fallacy

Most major pollsters still rely on cold-calling landlines and cell numbers. Response rates have fallen below 6%, creating massive non-response bias and producing the kind of high-profile misses we've seen in recent election cycles.

🤖 The Bot Problem

Existing online panels have no reliable way to verify citizenship or residency. Bots, VPN users, and duplicate accounts corrupt data quality — and there's no economic incentive for respondents to be truthful.

✅ The Panel Opportunity

Voters are tired of feeling unheard. Panel transforms polling from an unwanted interruption into a paid, verified civic activity — giving every citizen a real voice and real money for sharing it.

03 — Data Independence

Free from Selection Bias.
Free from Advertisers.

Panel is completely independent — no media affiliation, no political agenda, no advertising revenue. This independence, combined with our random selection engine, is what makes our data trustworthy.

🎲 Random Selection, Not Opt-In

Traditional panels suffer from self-selection bias — the people who volunteer for surveys are systematically different from those who don't. Panel eliminates this by randomly selecting respondents from its verified user base for each poll. Users don't browse and choose which polls to take; they're selected and notified. If a batch doesn't fully respond, we pull another random batch until the sample is filled. This produces statistically representative samples by design.

🚫 Zero Advertisements, Ever

Panel will never run ads. Every feature on the platform — voting records, bill tracking, spending alerts, stock trade monitoring — is funded entirely from polling margins. No advertiser influence means no conflict of interest. Users trust the platform because it has no reason to manipulate their attention. This is a civic tool, not an ad delivery vehicle.

📊 Why This Matters for Data Quality

When a media outlet commissions a Panel poll, the results come from verified citizens who were randomly selected — not self-selected volunteers, not people who follow a particular news source, not users who opted into a panel because of political interest. The result: polling data that's structurally free from the selection biases that plague every existing methodology.
04 — User Growth Engine

Progressive Trust Ladder

A US Passport is the required first step — it's the only universally available, definitive proof of US citizenship. From there, users progressively unlock higher-paying local polls by adding more verification. No one registers as an independent contractor until they hit the $600 IRS reporting threshold.

🛂

Level 1 — National Citizen

User scans their US Passport via Stripe Identity. This is the required first step for all panelists — no other document can definitively prove US citizenship.

Unlocks: National Polls — e.g. "Presidential Approval"

🏠

Level 2 — Local Resident

User adds their Driver's License. Stripe verifies the ID and returns only state + zip code. Panel confirms identity continuity by matching a fuzzy name hash against the passport hash — the actual name is never stored.

Unlocks: State & Local Polls — e.g. "Virginia Governor Race"
A passport must already be on file before a DL can be added. This means national polling launches first, with state/local following as users level up.
⚡

Level 3 — Power Earner

Users are not required to provide their SSN or register as an independent contractor until their annual earnings reach $600 — the IRS reporting threshold. Until then, earnings flow freely with zero tax friction.

Required at $600/yr: SSN for 1099-NEC Compliance
This removes a major registration barrier. Most casual panelists will never hit $600/yr, keeping the onboarding funnel wide open.

🛂 Why require a passport?

US citizenship is virtually impossible to prove definitively without a passport. A Driver's License proves residency and identity, but not citizenship. A birth certificate is not a photo ID and is trivially forged. The passport is the only federally issued, photo-verified, machine-readable proof of citizenship available to all Americans — and anyone who doesn't have one can apply for one.

🚀 Rollout Strategy: National First

Because a passport is required before a DL can be added, Panel launches with national-scale polls from day one. State and local polling unlocks organically as users add Driver's License verification — creating a natural growth flywheel from broad national coverage to hyper-local precision.

How the Fuzzy Name Hash Works

When a user scans their passport, Stripe extracts the name and Panel generates a one-way fuzzy hash — a normalized, irreversible string that cannot be decoded back to the original name. When the same user later scans a Driver's License, Panel generates another fuzzy hash from the DL name and compares the two. If they match → same person, verified for local polls. If they don't → flagged for review. At no point does Panel store, see, or log the user's actual name.

🛡️ What happens if Panel gets breached?

An attacker would find: anonymous user IDs, one-way name hashes (irreversible), boolean citizenship tokens, zip codes, age brackets, and gender flags. No names, no document images, no SSNs, no addresses, no bank details. There is nothing in Panel's database that could be used for identity theft — because we architected it that way from day one.

🔀 Stripe Never Aggregates Your Documents

The passport verification call and the Driver's License verification call are completely separate API requests to Stripe Identity — made at different times, with different session tokens, under different verification contexts. Stripe processes each document independently and returns isolated results. There is no aggregation, no cross-referencing, and no unified identity profile being built on Stripe's side. The only place continuity is established between the two documents is via the fuzzy name hash that Panel generates locally — and that hash is a one-way, irreversible string that neither Stripe nor anyone else can decode back to a name.

📱 This Isn't New — People Scan IDs Every Day

Scanning government-issued IDs through a phone is not a novel or unusual action. Millions of Americans already do it routinely — to open bank accounts (Chase, Wells Fargo), activate trading platforms (Robinhood, Schwab), verify identity for gig work (Uber, DoorDash), access crypto exchanges (Coinbase), and more. In every one of these cases, the document image is transmitted to a third-party verification provider like Stripe, Jumio, or Onfido. Panel works the same way — except we go further by never retaining the verified data.

⚠️ Threat: Copycat / Honeypot Apps

A real risk is bad actors creating fake apps that look like Panel to harvest identity documents. We are mitigating this aggressively. Google now requires all Play Store apps to be signed with verified developer certificates, making it significantly harder to publish convincing clones. On our end, we will actively monitor app stores for look-alikes, issue takedown requests immediately, and educate users on how to verify they're using the authentic Panel app. We'll also maintain a public list of our official app store links and signing certificates.

🔓 Open Source + Remote Binary Attestation

To give users the ultimate peace of mind, we plan to make Panel's app and backend code fully open source. Anyone — security researchers, journalists, skeptical users — can audit exactly what the code does. But open source alone isn't enough (users need to know the app on their phone matches the public code), so we will also implement remote binary attestation: a cryptographic process that lets any user verify that the binary running on their device was compiled from the exact public source code — with no hidden modifications, no backdoors, and no secret data collection. This is the highest standard of trust an app can offer.

🎲 Random Selection Engine

Panel doesn't blast notifications to every eligible user. When a pollster defines their criteria — say, "US Women aged 45–55" — Panel randomly selects a batch from the matching pool and sends them a push notification. If only 500 of the initial 1,000 respond, Panel automatically selects and notifies another random batch from the remaining eligible pool. This process repeats until the poll's sample size is fully filled. Random selection eliminates self-selection bias (a major flaw in opt-in panels) and ensures every poll produces a statistically representative sample.

09 — Open Data

Public by Default.
Private at a Premium.

By default, every poll result is published openly on the app for all users to see — live as responses come in, and archived once complete. But for sponsors who need confidentiality, Panel offers a private polling tier at a higher price.

📡 Public Polls (Default)

Any user can browse recently completed polls or watch results come in live — even if they weren't selected to participate. Sponsors are free to use their results on air, in print, or in any publication. The public can independently verify every result on the app.

🔒 Private Polls (Premium)

Political strategists and campaigns can commission private polls whose results are only visible to the sponsor. The poll question is not displayed publicly, screenshots are disabled within the app to prevent social media leaks, and results never appear in the public feed. This lets campaigns gather intelligence without tipping off their competition.

🌐 Why Public by Default?

Transparency builds trust — both with users and with the broader public. When anyone can see every public poll, every methodology, and every result, there's no room for accusations of cherry-picked data or hidden findings. It also turns Panel into a destination: even users who aren't participating in a given poll have a reason to open the app and check what America is thinking right now. The private tier is a premium revenue stream, not the default mode.
10 — Data Integrity

Two Layers of Defense
Against Biased Data

Panel attacks data quality from both ends: we stop lazy responses with the Paraphrase Gate, and we eliminate sponsor bias by keeping poll funders anonymous until the results are in.

🕵️ Blind Sponsorship: You Don't Know Who's Asking

When a panelist receives a poll, they see the question, the bounty, and the demographic targeting — but not the sponsor. The funding organization is revealed only after the poll closes and results are published. This eliminates one of the most insidious forms of response bias in modern polling: the tendency for respondents to adjust their answers based on who they think is asking. A CNN-commissioned poll and a FOX News-commissioned poll on identical questions routinely produce different results — not because opinion differs, but because people calibrate their responses to the perceived audience. Panel makes that impossible by design. The sponsor sees honest answers. The public sees authentic data.

👤 What Panelists See (During)

A verified sponsor has placed a bounty. The question. The amount. The deadline. Nothing else. No logo, no outlet name, no hint of political leaning.

📢 What Everyone Sees (After)

Once the poll closes, the sponsor is revealed alongside the final results — publicly archived, fully transparent. Sponsors get honest data. The public gets accountability.

📊 Why This Makes Our Data Better

A traditional polling panel is just a pool of warm bodies. Panel's Power Users are verified citizens who have actively demonstrated knowledge of the government processes they're being asked about. When a pollster asks a question about Federal Reserve policy, the responses from Panel's Power User cohort carry a credibility that no other polling outfit can offer — and pollsters can filter for Power Users specifically, commanding higher bid prices for their responses.

🔥 The Engagement Flywheel

Streaks, XP bars, and milestone unlocks aren't gimmicks — they're proven engagement mechanics. Duolingo has demonstrated that gamification can sustain daily active use for language learning, one of the hardest habits to maintain. Civics is easier: shorter sessions, directly actionable knowledge, and a concrete financial reward at the finish line. Users who are working toward Power User status open the app daily. That means more poll responses per panelist, faster fill times for sponsors, and a compounding network effect as the educated cohort grows.

⚡ The 75% Split — A Sustainable Incentive

At the standard 50/50 split, Panel's margin funds the entire civic transparency platform with zero ads. The Power User tier reduces Panel's cut to 25% on those respondents — but this is intentional and sustainable. Power Users respond faster, pass the Paraphrase Gate at higher rates, and attract premium poll bids. A poll filled with Power Users is worth more to a sponsor than a general-population poll. Panel can charge accordingly, keeping the economics healthy even with the elevated payout.

13 — AI-Proof by Design

The Gig Economy Is About to
Lose Millions of Workers.
Panel Absorbs Them.

Autonomous vehicles are coming for Uber and Lyft drivers. AI-powered robots are coming for DoorDash and Instacart couriers. Within the next decade, millions of gig workers will be displaced by automation. Panel is one of the rare platforms that benefits from this transition.

🤖 Why AI Can't Replace Panelists

The entire point of Panel is to capture verified human opinion. An AI can drive a car, deliver groceries, and write code — but it cannot be a citizen with a valid passport, a genuine political opinion, and a verified residential address. Panel's value proposition is intrinsically human. The more AI automates everything else, the more valuable authentic human input becomes. Our product is literally the thing AI cannot fake.

🔄 Repurposing Displaced Gig Labor

When an Uber driver loses their gig to a Waymo, they still have a phone, they still need income, and they're already comfortable with app-based micro-earnings. Panel is a natural landing pad: no car required, no schedule, no physical labor — just scan your passport, answer questions from your couch, and get paid. As autonomous driving and delivery AI eliminates traditional gig work, Panel's potential labor pool doesn't shrink — it explodes.

📈 A Tailwind, Not a Headwind

Most startups are racing to figure out how AI will disrupt their business. Panel has the opposite problem: AI disruption in adjacent industries actively drives our growth. Every gig worker displaced by autonomy is a potential panelist. Every AI-generated deepfake and bot-flooded survey makes verified human opinion more scarce and more valuable. Panel sits at the intersection of these two mega-trends — rising supply of available labor and rising demand for authentic human data.
15 — Unit Economics

A Bid-Based Marketplace
with a Tiered Revenue Split

Panel operates as a marketplace with a $5.00 minimum bid per verified response. Standard panelists earn 50% of every bounty. Power Users — those who have completed the Civic Academy curriculum — earn 75%, incentivizing education and driving a more knowledgeable, premium respondent pool that commands higher sponsor bids.

⚡ Priority Queue

When a panelist has multiple pending poll invitations, the highest-bounty poll is delivered first. Sponsors who bid above the minimum naturally get faster fill rates — no special "priority" fee required. The marketplace self-optimizes: a $10 bounty poll will fill faster than a $5 one because panelists respond to money.

⏱️ Speed Guarantees (Premium)

For sponsors who need results on a hard deadline — breaking news polls, debate night reactions, election eve snapshots — Panel offers a guaranteed fill time (e.g., "1,000 responses in 30 minutes"). We achieve this by sending larger initial batches and more aggressive re-batching. Think of it like surge pricing: you're paying for immediacy.

📐 Why the Numbers Go Up

As the platform scales, average bids naturally rise. At launch, most polls are national with large eligible pools — $5 fills them easily. As state and local polls unlock, smaller pools mean more competition among sponsors for the same verified panelists, driving bids higher. At scale, breaking news and election-cycle demand creates surge pricing dynamics. The $5 floor is where we start — the market determines where it goes.
